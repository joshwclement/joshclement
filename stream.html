Streaming Fitness

Daily Burn produces fitness content. Our customers stream the conent into their living rooms and get fit. As we expanded onto new platforms (and screen sizes), our design system supporting that content started to fall apart.

Fonts, buttons, content, icons, functionality. Inconsistent and a bit rusty!

Goals

Responsive, scalable, consistent. These were a few of our design principles for the project.

We focused on two core screens. The workout detail screen, where users can see more info about a workout. Also the home view, where we show rows of relevant workouts.

Approach

How can our platforms share similar components?

What is the core function of the workout detail screen?

Iterating the metadata view on mobile screens.

Some platforms like Roku had hard constraints.

Workout selection interaction on Roku (prototype).

Built a new design system.

Results

Brand, visual and functional consistency across all our platforms. Better experience for our users, also a big decrease in maintenance time, and faster to launch new improvements. The app performs better, and it gives us a more flexible system for future features.

Responsive and consistent across all screens and devices.
